About Me

I'm currently enrolled as a student at Grand Circus within their After Hours Java Bootcamp and work full time as a manager in
Higher Education Foodservice at the University of Michigan.

I've been attracted to the tech field since I was very young; I still vividly remember the day a tech-savvy neighbor came to our house
to hook up our computer to the internet for the first time. It was so surreal to see it come to life in front of us! When I was 14 I found my way
into an IT internship (also at the University) and worked there for two summers as an IT Generalist/network admin.

When I turned 16 and got my license, I got a job as a dishwasher at Coney Island because it was 5 minutes from my house.
It seems like I blinked, and now here I stand as a leader, Certified Executive Chef and General Manager overseeing hundreds of
staff in multi-million dollar operations.

After hitting a glass ceiling in my current role, both in terms of growth and my scope of influence, I discovered an opportunity at
Grand Circus' bootcamp and decided to jump head first back into Tech head learning software development. By leveraging my professional
experiences dealing with customer-centric, and interpersonal relationship-dependency in high volume, complex systems
I hope to feed both my personal growth and curiosity, as well as my influence in others.

My long term, highest aspiration is to change the way humans interact with the world in lasting and meaningful ways. In the short term,
I am focused on diving deep into the craft and learning, hands-on, how to think and take decisive action as a top-tier developer.
